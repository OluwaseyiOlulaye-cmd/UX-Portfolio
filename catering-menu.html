Case study:
CateringMenu App Design

Project Overview

The Problem

Local caterers lack the time and the medium to quickly have an idea of current market prices of items needed to generate accurate and efficient quotation for their clients at the point of asking.

The goal:

Design an app for CateringMenu that allow users to have a idea of the current market prices to genereate quotation for clients, and plan efficiently for their catering orders.

The Product:

CateringMenu is a local catering technology business located in the central part of a metropolitan area. CateringMenu strives to provide an innovative platform for local caterers to get the current market prices of goods, generate quotation for their clients, order from fresh and cheap farm produce from verified local vendors.

My Role:

UX designer designing an app for CateringMenu from conception to delivery.

Responsibilities:

Conducting foundational interviews, paper and digital wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, and iterating on designs.

Project Duration:

June 2021 to August 2021.

Understanding the User

I conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand the users I’m designing for and their needs. A primary user group identified through research was commercial caterers (outdoor/indoor) who needs to have an efficient planning process to meet her customers order on time.

This user group confirmed initial assumptions lack of quick and easy access to current market prices , but research also revealed that time was not the only factor limiting users from order’s delivery. Other user problems included obligations, interests, or challenges that make it difficult to get/transport groceries for cooking or cooked food to the party venues; and access to a catering learning platform for more professional development.

User journey map

Mapping Akinbumiti’s user journey revealed how helpful it would be for users to have access to a dedicated CateringMenu App.

Starting the design

Paper wireframes

Taking the time to draft iterations of each screen of the app on paper ensured that the elements that made it to digital wireframes would be well-suited to address user pain points. For the home screen, I prioritized a quick and easy listings/ordering process to help users save time.

Digital wireframes

As the initial design phase continued, I made sure to base screen designs on feedback and findings from the user research.

Easy navigation was a key user need to address in the designs in addition to equipping the app to work with assistive technologies. A quick glance and access to current market items/prices and the local vendors is important

Usability study findings

I conducted two rounds of usability studies. Findings from the first study helped guide the designs from wireframes to mockups. The second study used a high-fidelity prototype and revealed what aspects of the mockups needed refining.

Round 1 findings

  • Users want quick access to current market price lists
  • Users need a way to generate online quotation
  • Users need a way to quickly access local vendors

Round 2 findings

  • Confused by the word "local vendor"
  • Has trouble generating online quotation
  • Has trouble figuring out what to do after knowing the current market price lists

Refining the design

Mockups

Early designs allowed for some customization, but after the usability studies, I revised the design so users see all the customization options when they first land on the screen.

The second usability study revealed frustration of how to generate their online quotation. to streamline this flow, I consolidated the “no quotation” and to the “Quotation” screen. I also added the terms and conditions for their clients to acknowledge to this screen.

Accessibility considerations

  • Screen readers

Provided access to users who are vision impaired through adding alt text to images for screen readers.

  • Icon

Used icons to help make navigation easier.

  • Images

Used detailed imagery for market items to help all users better understand the designs.

Refined designs

High-fidelity prototype

The final high-fidelity prototype presented cleaner user flows for access to current market items, generate online quotation and checkout. It also met user needs for a pickup or delivery option as well as more customization.

Going Forward

Takeaways

Impact:

The app makes users feel like CateringMenu app really thinks about how to meet their needs.

One quote from peer feedback: “Great job. I particularly like your color wheel and the design of the app made it so easy and quick to have an idea of market prices ! I would definitely use this app as a go-to for quick market prices and my online quotation generation”

What I learned:

The app makes users feel like CateringMenu app really thinks about how to meet their needs.

While designing the CateringMenu app, I learned that the first ideas for the app are only the beginning of the process. Usability studies and peer feedback influenced each iteration of the app’s designs.

I learned that to put users front and center and to meet their needs through design, I can’t afford to miss any step or process of the design thinking.

Next steps

Conduct another round of usability studies to validate whether the pain points users experienced have been effectively addressed

    Make any necessasy iterations to effectively addressed user’s pain points.
