Case study:
TalentTech Hub & Responsive Website

Project Overview

The Problem

21.7 million Nigerian Youth between the ages of 15-24 remain unemployed. The strategy team at TalentTech hub has identified that a lack of talent development and digital skills needed to be gainfully employed.

And the lack of growth mindset required to thrive in this digital economy is crucial drivers for continued unemployment.

The goal:

Design an app that will improve tutoring on web development and digital skills education through free courses for skills development.

The Product:

TalentTech Hub is an African-based organization focused on talent and digital skills development against unemployment for teens and youths. The organization needs a tool that helps young adults learn about web development and other digital skills of interest.

TalentHub’s primary target users include African teenagers and young adults who are interested in learning web development/digital skills to be empowered with skills for income and global impact.

My Role:

UX designer leading the app and responsive website design from conception to delivery. .

Responsibilities:

Conducting interviews through the use of strategic research, paper and digial wireframing,low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usabilty studies, accounting for accessibility, iterating on designs, determing information architecture, and responsive design. .

Project Duration:

June 2021 to September 2021 .

Understanding the User

I developed short survey questions targeted at young adult on how we can fight against the lack of digital skills which makes African youths unqualified for jobs/entrepreneurship opportunities both in the local and international space. All interview participants indicated an interest in learning digital skills and web development, but challenges such as lack of access to high-tech tools, the timing/schedule, lack of free access to internet connection and jargon-filled courses prevents them from learning.. The feedback received through research made it clear that users would be open and willing to learn web development/digital skills if they have access to a free, personalized timing and accessible digital courses with a simplified approach. .

Personas and problem statement

Boluwatife

Boluwatife is a young undergraduate who wants to sign up to learn less-technical, comprehensive digital skills because she wants to be able to comprehend, understand and put those skills into practice.

Margaret

Margaret is a soon-to-be graduate who wants to sign up for a free access to an e-learning digital platform because she desire to learn skills that will get her empowered and equipped against unemployment.

Francis

Francis is a young adult who is interested in e-learning of digital skills and wants to gain professional experience because he wants to be equipped to make money

Competitive audit

An audit of a few competitor’s products provided direction on gaps and opportunities to address with the TalentTech Hub app.

Ideation

I did a quick ideation exercise to come up with ideas for how to address gaps identified in the competitive audit. My focus was specifically on free, simplified, responsive and personalized web development trainings.

Starting the Design

Digital wireframes

After ideating and drafting some paper wireframes of 5 different options screen, I created the initial designs for the TalentTech Hub app. Theses designs focused on delivering an accessible, simplified, easy-to-understand and mobile-responsive courses to users to help learn digital skills/web development

Usability study findings

To prepare for testing, I created a low-fidelity prototype which you can view here. I used this prototype to conduct an unmoderated usability study with 6 participants. Here are the main findings uncovered by the usability study:

Tech-related articles

People want easy to be able to read tech-related beginner articles on the app.

Change Course

People want to be able to change their course if they so wish

Personalized learnings

People desire to be able to personlaise their learnings based on their schedule in case of unforseen circumstances

Refining the design

Mockups

Based on the insights from the usability studies, I applied design changes to the tech-related/career articles page provide a clear list and easy access to articles tailored to user’s need. .

Additional changes to the dashboard page provided users with clear and easy access to change their courses and make personalization based on their schedule and time .

Accessibility considerations

  • Screen readers

Clear labels for interactive elements that can be read by screen readers. .

  • Icon

Language translation icon allows other language for users others than English .

  • IA

Initial focus of the home screen on personalized recommendations help define the primary task or action for the user .

Responsive Design

Sitemap

With the app designs completed, I started work on designing the responsive website. I used the TalentTech Hub sitemap to guide the organizational structure of each screen’s design to ensure a cohesive and consistent experience across devices.

Responsive designs

The designs for screen size variation included mobile, tablet, and desktop. I optimized the designs to fit specific user needs of each device and screen size.

Going Forward

Takeaways

Impact:

Users shared that the app made web development and digital skills seem like something they could actually learn – as they could personalize their learnings and learn from the comfort of their home.

One quote from peer feedback: “One quote from peer feedback was that “the TalentTech Hub app helps me learn with my phone online and it gives me a more interactive and easy-to-understand courses with dedicated mentors.”
