Case study:
Virtuous Volunteer Website Design

Project Overview

The Problem

Availabe volunteer-based organizations lack the understanding of what motivates people to volunteer – and so they fail to put their needs into considerations making the registration process long, intimidating and unfulfilling .

The goal:

Design a volunteer registration flow for a humanitarian cause to be user friendly by providing a mobile friendly, less demanding, quick and fast registration process and fast response time. .

The Product:

Virtuous Volunteer is an African-based social enterprise organization that offers passionate volunteers an easy and intuitive way to offer thier skills, service and time to non-profit organizations. Virtuous Volunteer strives to provide a less rigorous but effective volunteer recruitment whilst nurturing the needs of volunteers. .

My Role:

UX designer designing an app for Virtuous Volunteer Responsive Website from conception to delivery.

Responsibilities:

Conducting foundational interviews, paper and digital wireframes, low and high-fidelity prototyping, conducting usability studies, accounting for accessibility, and iterating on designs.

Project Duration:

August 2021 – October 2021 .

Understanding the User

I conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand the users I’m designing for and their needs. A primary user group identified through research was young people who needs a mobile-friendly, quick registration process and fast response time to their application. .

This user group confirmed initial assumption that the application process takes time , but research also revealed that time was not the only factor limiting users from volunteering their services. Other user problems included lack of response, and demanding application process with unnecessary questions which could be answered later when their application to the organization is successful – it becomes more frustrating when after all the long process of application, their application wasn’t considered successful. .

User pain points

  1. Time

Intending volunteers lack the time to fill long application forms.

  1. Experience

Lack of feedback from nonprofit organizations on the status of the volunteers application makes the experience not user-friendly.

  1. Interaction

Long and demanding volunteer application forms makes the process more difficult for intending volunteers.

Starting the design

Sitemap

Difficulty with the volunteer registration form was a primary pain point for users, so I used that knowledge to create a sitemap to guide the organizational structure of each screen’s design to ensure a simple, quick and consistent experience across devices.

Paper wireframes

Taking the time to draft iterations of each screen of the app on paper ensured that the elements that made it to digital wireframes would be well-suited to address user pain points. The home screen paper variations below prioritized an optimized browsing experience and easy navigation for users.

Screen size variations

Because intending volunteers access the site on a variety of different devices especially on mobile, I started to work on designs for additional screens to make sure the site would be fully responsive .

Digital wireframes

Wireframes

Moving from paper to digital wireframes made it easy to understand how the redesign could help address user pain points and improve the user experience.  .

Screen size variations

Prioritizing easy navigation and visual element placement on the home page was a key part of my strategy.

Usability study findings

To start testing the designs, I created a low-fidelity prototype which you can view here. This prototype was used in an unmoderated usability study with 5 participants. Here are the main findings uncovered by the usability study: .

  • Form submission

Users find it difficult to know if their application has been submitted successfully

  • Navigation

Users find it difficult to navigate to have a quick idea about the organization’s program

  • Mobile responsiveness

Users want to be able to fill the form quickly using their mobile phones.

Refining the design

Mockups

Based on the insights from the usability study, I made changes to improve the site’s navigations and buttons. One of the changes I made was adding the option to click on the button “Learn More”. This allowed users easy access to the programs of the non-profit organization.

The second usability study revealed frustration of how to generate their online quotation. to streamline this flow, I consolidated the “no quotation” and to the “Quotation” screen. I also added the terms and conditions for their clients to acknowledge to this screen.

Accessibility considerations

  • Screen readers

Provided access to users who are vision impaired through adding alt text to images for screen readers.

  • Icon

Used icons to help make navigation easier.

  • Images

Used detailed imagery to help all users better understand the designs.

Going Forward

Takeaways

Impact:

Our target users shared that the design was intuitive, easy to navigate through, more engaging with the organizations programs, and a clear visual hierarchy was demonstrated. .

What I learned:

Throughout the project duration and design process I came to fully understand the true meaning of creativity and problem solving. I learned how to solve problems through fixed focus on the user – this made coming up with ideas and solutions easier.
